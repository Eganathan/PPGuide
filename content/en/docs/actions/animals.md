# Animals Action

Manage individual animals within your dairy cattle livestock.

## Purpose
Track detailed information about each animal including breed, health, breeding, lineage, and milk production.

## Availability

> **Important:** This action is only available under specific conditions:
> - Module must be **Livestock**
> - Animal type must be **Cattle**
> - Raised For must be **Dairy**
> - **Track Individually** must be turned ON
>
> If you don't see this action, check that all conditions above are met in your livestock settings.

## Key Features
- Individual animal profiles with name/ID
- Breed and gender tracking
- Date of birth recording
- Growth stage monitoring
- Breeding cycle tracking (Open, Bred, Pregnant)
- Milking status (Dry, Milking)
- Lactation number tracking
- Sire (father) and Dam (mother) records
- Mark animals as Sold or Deceased

## Animal Details

### Basic Information
- Name/ID
- Breed
- Gender
- Date of Birth
- Growth Stage

### Breeding Cycle
Track reproductive status of female animals:
- **Open** - Not bred, ready for breeding
- **Bred** - Mated but not confirmed pregnant
- **Pregnant** - Confirmed pregnancy

### Milking Status
Track current milk production status:
- **Dry** - Not producing milk
- **Milking** - Actively producing milk

### Lactation Number
Track how many lactation cycles the animal has completed.

### Parentage
- **Sire** - Father of the animal
- **Dam** - Mother of the animal

### Animal Status
- **Active** - Currently in the herd
- **Sold** - Animal was sold
- **Deceased** - Animal has died

## Tips
- Give each animal a unique identifier for easy tracking
- Record parentage to track lineage and make breeding decisions
- Update growth stages as animals mature
- Use this for dairy cattle to track milk yield per animal

## Best Practices
- Create animal records at birth or purchase
- Keep vaccination and health records updated
- Document breeding events promptly
- Track father and mother for genetic records
- Record milking status to identify productive animals
